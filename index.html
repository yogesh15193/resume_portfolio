About Yogesh Gupta

My name is Yogesh Gupta, and I am currently pursuing an MSc in Technology and Innovation Management at TU Dublin, where I hold multiple leadership and participatory roles. I serve as the Class Representative and Student Representative for the Faculty of Business, advocating for student concerns and fostering collaboration between the student body and faculty. Additionally, I am a Member of the Electoral Commission and an active member of the Debate Society, where I have participated in prestigious competitions like the Irish Times Debate.

My academic journey has been shaped by diverse experiences, beginning with a childhood influenced by my father’s postings as an Indian government official in London, Islamabad, and New York, allowing me to study across the globe . Transitioning back from the U.S. to India’s academically demanding environment at IIIT Delhi was a challenging experience that shaped my resilience and determination, but has made me more adaptable in learning about new environments, cultures and work arrangements, equipping me better to handle tough situations and challenges.

During my time at IIIT Delhi, I led Ambi+, a research-driven project focused on reducing ambulance response times in Delhi. Guided by Dr. Anand Srivastava, I developed algorithms, managed legal contracts, collaborated with 25 hospitals, and pitched the project to potential investors. Although the project did not launch due to funding challenges, it achieved technical milestones, including reducing simulated response times from 25 to 14 minutes, and earned me an A grade and a Letter of Recommendation.

Professionally, I have contributed to impactful projects that merge data engineering and problem-solving. At Sheroes, I co-developed a content recommendation engine prototype that advanced machine learning capabilities and boosted user engagement. Additionally, I implemented an NLP-based sentiment analysis tool and built data pipelines using Python frameworks like Scrapy and BeautifulSoup. At FenixWorks, I developed a project leveraging Wikipedia APIs to analyze company similarities within a dataset, generating actionable insights for strategic decision-making. The analysis considered various factors, including company size, location, industry, main sector of work, client base, target geography, and headquarters, enabling a robust comparison and improved decision-making processes.

As part of my master’s program, I’ve gained expertise in Finance, Innovation Management, and Project Management, along with practical knowledge of project charters, portfolio management, and financial modeling. Recognized with a Digital Badge for Entrepreneurship, I aim to bridge the gap between technology and management while driving impactful projects that foster growth and efficiency.
